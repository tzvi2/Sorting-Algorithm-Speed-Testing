Sorting Algorithm Speed Testing

Bubble sort starts from the beginning of an array, and compares the first two numbers. If the left number is greater than the right, the two numbers are swapped. We then compare the second and third numbers, and so on - until we reach the end when we start over from the beggining. If there were no swaps made in a given loop, the array is sorted.

To perform a quick sort algorithm we choose a number from the array to be a pivot. We'll then use two pointers to travel through the array, comparing numbers to the pivot, and placing numbers lower to the left, and higher to the right. After All the numbers were visited by the pointers once, we recurse on each side of the pivot element, choosing a new pivot and comparing numbers to that pivot.
